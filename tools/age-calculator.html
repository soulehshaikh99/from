4. Running Age Vs Completed Age

4.1 Running Age

The numbers of full years you've lived since your birth date.

For example, Angel was born on January 26, 2000. On January 26, 2025, she completes exactly 25 years. So, her completed age is 25.

4.2 Completed Age

The year of life you're in. This is the age you're currently living.

For example, if today is Angel's birthday, then she turned 25, but she has already started living her 26th year. So, her running age is 26.

You can calculate the same with a simple formula: Running Age = Completed Age + 1

5. How old am I in USA, or South Korea, or Middle East?

5.1 International Age (Western):

Most countries worldwide recognize the Gregorian calendar, which is based on the solar dating system. This calendar has 12 months and generally 365 days, with one leap year every four years (366 days). If your country follows this system, then your age is counted based on the number of birthdays you've celebrated so far. Your completed age is recorded on all legal or official documents, while the months and days in between are treated as just fractional parts of your age.

5.2 Islamic Age (Middle East):

Many countries in the Islamic World follows the Hijri calendar. It comprises of 12 months and follows the movements of the moon, making it a Lunar Calendar. The years are shorter in comparison to the Gregorian calendar as every month consists of only 29 or 30 days. If your country follows both the calendars, then your Islamic Age might be slightly ahead of your Western Age.

5.3 East Asian Age Reckoning:

Several East Asian countries traditionally follow the Chinese calendar, which is a lunisolar system similar to the Hebrew calendar. A regular calendar year has 12 months, while a leap year has 13 months. As per this counting system, you are considered one year old at birth. Your age increases collectively with everyone else on the Lunar New Year, not on your birthday. If your country follows this custom, then your child will be one year at birth and if the Lunar New Year falls just a week later, then your child would be considered two years old immediately after the New Year.

6. Will my age change if my time zone changes?

In short: NO.

Flying to a different time zone is not equivalent to time-travelling. You won't magically age an extra day or less. At most, it will push your birthday moment to a different local day.

10. Other Methods to Calculate Age

10.1 Fun Math tricks to Guess Someone's Age Without a Calculator

If you enjoy playing with numbers or just want to get the gears of your head rolling, then we have the perfect exercise for you. Use these tricks to impress your friends and family, or simply to improve your mental math speed.

10.1.1 Find your friend's age using a random number

  • Tell your friend to choose a number between 2 and 10.
  • Once they select a number, ask them what it is. Suppose they select the number 4.
  • Multiply that number by 2. For example, 4 x 2 = 8.
  • Add 5 to the answer. For example, 8 + 5 = 13.
  • Multiply that further by 50. For example, 13 x 50 = 650.

  • Now, there will be two scenarios.

    Case 1: If your friend's birthday has already passed in the current year, add 1767.

    Case 2: If your friend's birthday is still yet to arrive, add 1766.

  • Please note that this trick was compatible with the year 2017, so to make up for that gap, add a number for every plus year in the original 1767 or 1766. For example, if we assume the year is 2025, then it will be 1767 + 8 = 1775 or 1774 respectively.
  • With these additions, your final calculation will be 650 + 1775 = 2,425 or 2,424.
  • Lastly, subtract this answer with the year your friend was born in. For example, 2,425 - 1997 = 428.

There you have it!

Upon close analysis, you will find the digit in the hundredth place (4) is the number your friend originally chose in the beginning. The last two digits (28) reveal his current age.

Fun, right? Let's try another trick!

10.1.2 Find how old you'll be in future.

  • Multiply your current age by 2. For example, 28 x 2 = 56
  • Add your birth year. For example, 56 + 1997 = 2,053
  • Please note that this trick has limitations and the answer will always be the year when you turn double your current age.

You will be 56 years old in the year 2053.

10.1.3 Check your age on December 31 of any year - past or future.

  • Suppose you want to calculate age for December 31, 2035.
  • Subtract your birth year from that year. For example, 2035 - 1997 = 38.

Your age on December 31 of 2035 will be 38 years.
